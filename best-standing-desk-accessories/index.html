A standing desk is the foundation, but a handful of inexpensive accessories are what turn it into a workspace you genuinely enjoy. The right add-ons fix the things a desk alone cannot: a screen that is too low, cables that tangle every time you stand, a keyboard at the wrong height, a desk you wish you could move. Here are the upgrades that deliver the most for the least.

Most impactful: a monitor arm

If you add one accessory, make it a monitor arm. It lifts your screen to true eye level, which is the difference between a sustainable posture and a sore neck, and it stays at the right height whether you are sitting or standing. It also frees the chunk of desktop your monitor stand used to occupy. We cover the best options in depth in our monitor arm round-up, but the short version is that the Ergotron LX is the one to beat.

Best for cables: an under-desk cable tray

This is the accessory people forget until their first week with a moving desk, when they realize every cable now travels up and down with the desktop. An under-desk cable management tray mounts beneath the surface and corrals your power strip and cables so they rise and fall with the desk instead of yanking on the wall or pooling on the floor. Pair it with a vertical cable spine for the run down to the floor. Cheap, unglamorous, and transformative for how tidy your setup looks and feels.

Best for wrists: an under-desk keyboard tray

Even a perfectly set desk can leave your keyboard a touch too high for a truly neutral wrist angle. An under-desk keyboard tray clamps below the surface and drops your keyboard and mouse to exact elbow height, with many offering a slight negative tilt that keeps your wrists straight. If you type all day and want to protect your wrists over the long haul, this is a smart, low-cost addition.

Best for mobility: heavy-duty casters

If you ever want to reposition your desk, to clean behind it, rearrange the room, or chase the light, a set of locking desk casters makes it effortless. They swap in for the standard feet on most frames and lock firmly in place so the desk stays put while you work. Confirm compatibility and weight rating with your specific frame, but for the freedom to roll a heavy desk around, they are well worth it.

A few more worth considering

  • Anti-fatigue mat: arguably essential, not optional. See our mat round-up; it is the cheapest fix for sore feet while standing.
  • Headphone hook: a tiny under-desk hook keeps your headset off the surface and within reach.
  • Power strip with USB: mount one to the cable tray so everything charges from a single, tidy hub that moves with the desk.
  • Monitor light bar: a screen-mounted light reduces eye strain in a dim room without the glare of a desk lamp.
  • Desk pad: a large felt or leather mat protects the surface, defines your mouse area, and looks sharp.
Buy in this order. Monitor arm first (posture), then anti-fatigue mat (comfort), then cable tray (sanity), then the rest. Each one fixes a problem you will feel within your first week of standing.

The bottom line

A great standing setup is a desk plus a few well-chosen accessories. Start with a monitor arm to fix your posture, add an anti-fatigue mat so you can stand comfortably, tame your cables with an under-desk tray, and consider a keyboard tray and casters to round it out. None of these cost much individually, and together they are the difference between a desk you tolerate and one you love. New to all this? Begin with our standing desk buying guide.
