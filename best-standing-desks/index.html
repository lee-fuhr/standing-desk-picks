Sitting for hours on end is bad for your back, your energy, and your focus. A standing desk lets you switch between sitting and standing throughout the day, which can reduce stiffness and keep you more alert. But not all standing desks are built alike: stability at standing height, motor noise, adjustment range, and desktop quality vary widely. To choose the right desk, think about your height, your monitor setup, and whether you want extra features like memory presets or cable management. If you are taller or shorter than average, pay close attention to the height range. If you often move your desk, consider weight capacity and frame sturdiness. Frequently asked questions

How stable should a standing desk be?

At standing height (roughly 44–46 inches for most people) there should be minimal wobble when you type or nudge the surface. Test by extending to full height and pressing the corner, acceptable is a brief dampened movement, not sustained shaking that disrupts your monitor.

How much weight can a standing desk hold?

Most quality standing desks are rated for 150–300 lbs. For a typical setup, monitors, laptop, peripherals, you'll use well under 80 lbs. Weight capacity only becomes relevant if you have a very large multi-monitor rig or unusually heavy equipment.

What height range do I need?

For a seated position: desk height roughly equals elbow height when seated. For standing: elbow height when standing with relaxed shoulders. A good electric desk spans roughly 24–50 inches, which covers nearly all adults. If you're very tall or very short, check the specific model's range before buying.

Are single-motor or dual-motor standing desks better?

Dual-motor desks are more stable, lift faster, and handle more weight. Single-motor desks are cheaper and adequate for lighter loads. For most home offices with one or two monitors, either works, dual motor becomes important for heavier commercial setups.

How long does assembly take?

Most standing desks take 45–90 minutes to assemble solo. Vari is an exception at 15 minutes due to partial pre-assembly. If you hate DIY furniture, budget time (and potentially a second person) for the frame attachment and cable management.
