A monitor arm is the single highest-leverage ergonomic upgrade most people can make, and it is cheap relative to its impact. It lifts your screen to true eye level so you stop craning your neck, frees the desktop the monitor stand used to hog, and lets you reposition your display effortlessly when you switch between sitting and standing. On a sit-stand desk in particular, an arm is close to essential. Here are the ones worth buying.

Best overall: Ergotron LX

The Ergotron LX is the arm against which all others are judged. Its constant-force gas-spring mechanism moves smoothly with a fingertip and holds your monitor exactly where you put it, with no drift and no drama. The build is aluminum, the motion is buttery, and Ergotron backs it with a long warranty that reflects how durable these are, many are still working flawlessly years on. It costs more than the no-name arms, but it is the one you buy once and forget about. For most people, this is the arm to get.

Best value: HUANUO Single Arm

The HUANUO Single Monitor Arm delivers a genuinely good gas-spring experience for a fraction of the Ergotron’s price. The motion is not quite as refined and the materials are a step down, but it holds a single monitor securely, adjusts easily, and includes both clamp and grommet mounts. For a one-monitor setup on a budget, it is an easy recommendation and a massive upgrade over a desktop stand.

Best dual arm: Ergotron LX Dual

The Ergotron LX Dual brings the same excellence to two-screen setups, mounting both monitors on a single sturdy base so you can arrange them side by side or stack them. Two-monitor stability is harder to achieve than single, and this is where cheaper dual arms sag or wobble. The Ergotron does not. If you run two displays and want them to feel like one solid, adjustable unit, this is the pick.

Best dual value: VIVO Dual Mount

VIVO makes some of the most popular budget mounting hardware around, and its dual monitor desk mount is a sensible way to float two screens without spending Ergotron money. It is heavier-handed and less refined in its adjustments, but it is stable enough for everyday use and reclaims a lot of desk space. A solid value choice for dual monitors on a budget.

What to check before you buy

Confirm VESA and weight first. Your monitor needs VESA mounting holes (most do, commonly 75x75 or 100x100mm) and must fall within the arm’s weight and size range. Heavy or ultrawide monitors need a heavier-duty arm.
  • VESA pattern: check your monitor supports VESA mounting and which size. Some thin or budget monitors lack VESA holes entirely.
  • Weight and screen size rating: match the arm to your monitor. An underrated arm will droop; ultrawides and large panels need beefier arms.
  • Clamp versus grommet: a C-clamp grips the desk edge; a grommet mount bolts through a hole. Confirm your desk works with one of them, and that a sit-stand frame leaves room for the clamp.
  • Range of motion: for sit-stand use you want generous height travel so the screen stays at eye level in both positions.

The bottom line

If you buy one upgrade for your desk after the desk itself, make it a monitor arm. The Ergotron LX is the do-it-once-and-forget-it choice for a single screen, the LX Dual for two, and the HUANUO and VIVO arms deliver most of the benefit on a budget.
